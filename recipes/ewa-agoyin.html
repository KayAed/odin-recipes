Ewa Agoyin and Fried Plantain

A plate containing boiled beans and sauce (popularly called Ewa Agoyin), paired with finely fried plantains.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups honey beans
  • 6 cups water
  • 1/2 - 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup shombo (reduce quantity if you don't like it spicy).
  • 3 large tatashe/bell peppers
  • 2 onions
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 maggi crayfish
  • 1 bouillon cube
  • 1/4 cup crayfish
  • plantain
  • Cooking Oil

Steps

  1. To an empty pot, add your rinsed beans, water and some salt. Leave to boil for about 30 - 40 minutes until it is soft.
  2. While it is cooking, soak your Shombo/dried chili in water and let it sit for about 30 minutes to 1 hour
  3. Once that is done, in your blender cup/food processor, add the strained chilis and onions, then blend.
  4. To a heated pan, add some palm oil and let it heat up.
  5. To your heated oil, add some chopped onions and fry for about 2-3 minutes.
  6. After that, add your blended mix, then add 1 maggi crayfish and 1 chicken mix to taste.
  7. Keep frying the sauce and keep stiring so it doesn't burn, for about 10 minutes.
  8. After 10 minutes, add the crayfish powder/pounded crayfish and fry for about 8-10 minutes, after which it would be much darker.
  9. Turn off the heat and check on your beans. It should be ready.
  10. Fry your plantains, serve up your beans, add the sauce, add your plantains and enjoy!
