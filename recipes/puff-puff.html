Beautifully fried Puff puff

Puff puff is a Nigrian snack that is enjoyed on its own, but forms part of a popular party snack gang called Small chops.

This recipe gives you a quick, light and non-oily snack at the end.

Ingredients

  • 3 Cups of Flour
  • 1 1/5 Tsps of yeast
  • 3/4 Cups of Sugar
  • 2 Cups of Water
  • Salt
  • Cooking Oil

Steps

  1. Sift your flour into a bowl.
  2. To the flour, add 1 1/5 teaspoons of yeast.
  3. To this mixture, add in your sugar and some pinch of salt.
  4. Mix to combine.
  5. Add a cup of water to this mix and start mixing gently with your spatula. Gradually add more water as needed until a thick, but slightly runny barter is formed.
  6. Once properly mixed, cover the top of the bowl with a cling film, then wrap the entire bowl in towels.
  7. Keep in a warm and let it sit for at least 30 minutes.
  8. Once the time is up or you find that it has doubled in size, mix once again.
  9. To your cooking pot/frying pan, add some oil - enough to deep fry a round egg.
  10. Once your oil is hot enough, (you can test this by dipping a skewer into the oil. If bubbles form around it after some seconds, your oil is ready. Alternatively, you could add a tiny drop of the puff puff barter. If the floats to the top, your oil is ready.).
  11. Oil your clean palms and scoop some, then create a hole using your thumb and the rest of your fingers, sliding your thumb over the hole to cut the barter into the oil.
  12. Repeat the process, but be careful not to crowd your frying pan. Fry till golden brown
  13. Take it off the heat, allow excess oil (if any,) to drain, then Enjoy!
